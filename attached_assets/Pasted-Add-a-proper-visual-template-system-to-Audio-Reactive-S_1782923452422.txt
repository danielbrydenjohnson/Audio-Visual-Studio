TEMPLATE 2: ORBITAL SWARM

Create a 3D orbital particle system.

Base structure:

- Create between three and five invisible attractor points
- Each attractor slowly moves through 3D space
- Each particle is assigned to an attractor
- Each particle has its own orbital radius
- Each particle has its own orbit speed
- Each particle has its own orbit direction
- Each particle has its own inclination and phase
- Orbits must not all sit on the same flat plane
- The combined result should look like several organic particle swarms

Do not render the attractor points themselves.

Low reaction:

- Low-sensitive particles receive short radial impulses
- Some move temporarily closer to their attractor
- Some move temporarily farther away
- Individual particle sizes may increase briefly
- Do not expand every orbit simultaneously

Mid reaction:

- Mid-sensitive particles receive individual changes to orbital curvature
- Add local turbulence and phase changes
- Different particles should bend differently
- Do not rotate the full scene or every orbit together

High reaction:

- High-sensitive particles sparkle independently
- A changing subset should become brighter or slightly larger
- Add restrained short velocity disturbances
- Avoid full-screen flashing

The result should feel alive and fluid, not like a rigid solar system.

TEMPLATE 3: PULSE TUNNEL

Create a deep cylindrical particle tunnel.

Base structure:

- Arrange particles throughout a long 3D cylindrical volume
- Keep the camera looking down the tunnel
- Particles should move toward the camera at different speeds
- When particles pass the camera, recycle them to the far end
- Give every particle its own:
  - angle
  - tunnel radius
  - z position
  - forward speed
  - phase
  - wave amount
  - Low affinity
  - Mid affinity
  - High affinity

The tunnel should have genuine perspective and depth.

Do not construct the tunnel from rigid identical rings.

Particles should be distributed organically rather than forming a perfect grid.

Low reaction:

- Low-sensitive particles individually accelerate toward the camera
- Some particles briefly increase in size
- Some particles receive small radial impulses
- Do not move the entire tunnel forward at one speed
- Do not scale the complete tunnel

Mid reaction:

- Mid-sensitive particles receive individual lateral wave motion
- Use each particle's phase and direction
- Create local bending and twisting through individual particle positions
- Do not rotate the entire tunnel

High reaction:

- High-sensitive particles sparkle individually
- Add short brightness peaks
- Allow a changing subset to create brief streak-like motion
- Do not flash the complete scene
