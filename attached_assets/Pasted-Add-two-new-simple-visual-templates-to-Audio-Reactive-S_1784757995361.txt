Add two new simple visual templates to Audio Reactive Studio.

The current app is working. It has uploaded track mode, live input mode, Low/Mid/High level and hit analysis, Hit Response controls, collapsible UI sections, visual templates, Kaleidoscope, post effects, recording, and output settings.

Do not rebuild the app from scratch.

Do not remove or replace existing templates.

Do not change recording.

Do not change Kaleidoscope.

Do not change post effects.

GOAL

Add two new simple, elegant templates based on a small number of curvy / wiggly luminous lines.

These templates should be calmer and more minimal than the denser geometric templates.

They should look intentional and premium, not like placeholders.

NEW TEMPLATE 1

Name:
Silk Lines

Description:
"A minimal set of luminous flowing lines drifting gently through space."

Concept:
A small number of curvy, wiggly lines with subtle movement.

They should feel like luminous ribbons or threads suspended in space.

NEW TEMPLATE 2

Name:
Orbital Threads

Description:
"Flowing luminous lines with small spheres anchored at their ends."

Concept:
Similar to Silk Lines, but each line should have a small sphere at both ends.

The spheres should visually anchor the line and make the form feel more designed.

The spheres should not be huge.

They should be small, elegant, and clearly connected to the line ends.

They should move naturally with the lines.

GENERAL VISUAL REQUIREMENTS FOR BOTH

Both templates should:

- be clean and minimal
- have only a few long smooth curvy lines
- have small differences in shape, offset, and phase
- have slight depth separation
- have gentle resting movement when audio is paused
- remain readable and calm
- look strong in Monochrome
- work well with Kaleidoscope
- work well with Bloom when enabled
- still look good when Post Effects are off

These should not be:

- particle clouds
- dense lattices
- tunnels
- corridors
- chaotic scribbles

DENSITY MAPPING

Both templates should use the existing Visual Density control, but with a simple stepped mapping:

- Low density = 3 lines
- Medium density = 6 lines
- High density = 9 lines

For Orbital Threads, that means:

- Low density = 3 lines and 6 spheres
- Medium density = 6 lines and 12 spheres
- High density = 9 lines and 18 spheres

Do not create excessive geometry.

If the existing density setting is numeric, map it internally for these templates to the three tiers above.

MOVEMENT

Both templates should have subtle resting motion even with no audio.

Examples of acceptable motion:

- slight wave undulation
- slow drifting
- gentle phase movement
- small depth drift
- slight organic wriggle

The templates should remain calm and elegant.

Do not let them become chaotic.

Do not let the motion become fast or noisy by default.

For Orbital Threads:

- the end spheres should follow the line ends naturally
- they may have slight additional shimmer or emphasis
- they should not detach from the line ends
- they should feel like part of one connected structure

AUDIO MAPPING

Use the existing Low / Mid / High level and hit signals.

Keep the reactions tasteful and restrained.

LOW

For both templates:
- create slight heavier pulses in selected lines
- some lines can sway outward slightly
- subtle brightness or scale punch if possible
- do not move all lines identically

For Orbital Threads:
- the end spheres can get a subtle punch or forward emphasis on Low
- keep it restrained

MID

For both templates:
- Mid should be the main expressive band
- create controlled ripple / wiggle modulation along the lines
- keep it bounded and elegant
- do not let Mid make the lines go mad

For Orbital Threads:
- the spheres can echo the line motion slightly
- avoid excessive wobble or chaos

HIGH

For both templates:
- add small brightness accents, glints, or local shimmer along line segments
- a changing subset of line portions can brighten briefly
- avoid full-scene flashing

For Orbital Threads:
- the small spheres can sparkle slightly on High
- small glints are good
- do not turn them into flashing bulbs

As with all templates:

- individual line behaviour should vary
- the whole scene should not simply scale as one object
- the whole scene should not rotate as one object
- at 0% influence, that band should have no visible effect
- resting animation should continue even when influences are 0%

VISUAL STYLE

Default palette should work especially well in:

- Monochrome

Also support:

- Cyan Violet
- Ember

Monochrome should feel clean and premium.

IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

Use efficient Three.js techniques.

Good options include:

- one or a small number of line geometries
- BufferGeometry
- Line
- Points or small sphere meshes where appropriate
- typed arrays
- shared materials
- a controlled number of objects

For Orbital Threads:
- small sphere meshes are acceptable because the count is low
- keep the sphere count controlled and efficient
- spheres should remain attached to line endpoints

Do not create excessive object counts.

Do not use React state updates inside requestAnimationFrame.

Do not create a new renderer, scene, camera, or animation loop.

TEMPLATE ARCHITECTURE

Preserve the existing template architecture:

- one persistent renderer

- one scene
- one camera

Register both templates cleanly in:

- template type / ID union
- template metadata

Suggested template IDs:

- silk-lines

Do not break existing templates.

Both templates must work with:

- Kaleidoscope on / off
- Kaleidoscope segments
- Kaleidoscope rotation
- Post Effects off
- Bloom on
- uploaded track mode
- live input mode
- MP4 recording

- 720p
