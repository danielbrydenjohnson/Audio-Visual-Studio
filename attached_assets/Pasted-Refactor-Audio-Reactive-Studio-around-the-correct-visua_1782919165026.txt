Refactor Audio Reactive Studio around the correct visual principle.

The current connected 2D particle visual and four-band audio model are conceptually wrong.

The product should use a genuine 3D particle field where individual particles react differently to three audio frequency bands:

- Low
- Mid
- High

Do not rebuild the audio upload, playback, seeking, file replacement, application shell, or general layout.

This task should replace the current particle renderer, update the audio-analysis model from four bands to three, and update the related controls and types.

CORE PRODUCT PRINCIPLE

The particle field must not react as one solid object.

Do not use any of the following as the main audio reaction:

- Scaling the entire scene
- Scaling the whole particle cloud
- Expanding the whole cloud from the centre
- Rotating the complete particle system
- Moving every particle in the same direction
- Flashing the entire canvas on every beat
- Connecting particles with lines

Each particle must have its own behaviour, audio affinity, movement phase, size, brightness, position, and velocity.

The visual should feel like particles floating through real 3D space, closer to Trapcode Particular than a network graph.

Replace the existing Sub, Low, Mid, and High model with exactly three bands:

Low:
- Frequency range: 20 Hz to 250 Hz
- Represents sub-bass, basslines, and kick energy

Mid:
- Frequency range: 250 Hz to 4,000 Hz
- Represents snares, claps, vocals, synth body, and melodic content

High:
- Frequency range: 4,000 Hz to 16,000 Hz
- Represents hi-hats, cymbals, brightness, and sharp transients

Update all related code:

- Frequency analysis types
- Frequency meter types
- Frequency meter display
- Audio-reactive settings types
- Default influence settings
- App.tsx state
- Particle renderer props
- Reset behaviour
- Labels in the interface

Remove every remaining reference to the old Sub band.

Keep analyser.fftSize at 2048.

Calculate the average energy of the FFT bins inside each of the three frequency ranges.

Display each band from 0 to 100.

Keep the raw analyser values unchanged by the visual influence sliders.

THREE-DIMENSIONAL PARTICLE RENDERER

Replace the existing HTML Canvas 2D particle renderer with a Three.js WebGL renderer.

Requirements:

- Add and use the `three` package directly
- Do not use React Three Fiber
- Use THREE.Points with BufferGeometry
- Do not create a separate Mesh for every particle
- Use a PerspectiveCamera
- Give every particle a real x, y, and z position
- Create a spacious 3D volume around and in front of the camera
- Make nearby particles appear larger and brighter
- Make distant particles appear smaller and dimmer
- Allow particles to move toward and away from the camera
- Recycle or wrap particles when they leave the defined 3D volume
- Use a very dark background
- Remove all connecting lines permanently

Use a custom ShaderMaterial if necessary for efficient per-particle size, colour, opacity, glow, depth, and audio reaction.

- Float slowly through 3D space
- Drift in different directions

- Pass toward and away from the camera
- Feel organic rather than mechanically uniform

The resting animation must not depend on audio playback.

INDIVIDUAL LOW REACTION

Low must affect Low-sensitive particles individually.

Possible reactions:

- Temporary increase in individual particle size
- Short movement impulses along each particle's own direction
- Faster movement through the z-axis
- Slight outward pressure based on each particle's personal position and affinity

Requirements:

- Particles with high Low affinity should react strongly
- Particles with low Low affinity should barely react
- Do not expand or scale the complete particle cloud
- Do not move every particle outward simultaneously
- Do not permanently alter the base particle positions
- The reaction should feel heavy and rhythmic

INDIVIDUAL MID REACTION

Mid must affect Mid-sensitive particles individually.

Possible reactions:

- Curved movement
- Local orbital movement
- Direction changes
- Turbulence
- Side-to-side movement
- Individual colour modulation

Requirements:

- Every reacting particle should use its own phase, direction, and affinity
- Do not rotate the complete particle scene
- Do not rotate one parent group containing all particles
- Do not make all particles turn at the same time
- The reaction should feel fluid and active rather than like a rigid spin

INDIVIDUAL HIGH REACTION

High must affect High-sensitive particles individually.

Possible reactions:

- Sparkle
- Short brightness peaks
- Opacity changes
- Small size flashes
- Brief velocity changes
- Rapid twinkling among a changing subset of particles

Requirements:

- Only some High-sensitive particles should flash strongly at any moment
- Use each particle's random seed, affinity, phase, and sparkle threshold
- Avoid making every particle flash together
- Avoid full-screen strobing
- Avoid a constant white overlay
- The reaction should feel sharp and detailed

SMOOTHING

Smooth Low, Mid, and High independently.

Use a fast attack and slower release for each band.

Suggested starting values:

- Attack coefficient: approximately 0.35
- Release coefficient: approximately 0.055

Keep all smoothed values inside the renderer or animation-loop closure.

Do not create React state updates inside requestAnimationFrame.

A paused track should cause all audio reactions to fade naturally back to zero while the base particle movement continues.
