TEMPLATE 1: SMILING FACE EMITTER

Create a recognisable smiling face shape.

This should not be a flat yellow emoji.

Visual idea:

A stylised face built from luminous line and particle geometry, with:

- circular or near-circular head outline
- two recognisable eyes
- a recognisable smiling mouth
- internal depth and dimension
- subtle 3D layering
- visible structure even when paused

The face should feel intentional and iconic.

Most importantly:

The smiling mouth should emit or spew particles outward.

This is the main signature behaviour.

Construction guidance:

- Head outline can be a ring, line loop, particle ring, or wireframe circle
- Eyes can be point clusters, ring nodes, or small geometric markers
- Mouth can be a curved line or segmented geometry
- The mouth should be the main particle emitter region
- Emitted particles should travel outward from the mouth region into space
- The emitted particles can dissipate, fade, or recycle

Base movement:

- The face structure should hold together and remain recognisable
- Small resting motion is allowed in the outline and facial features
- Mouth-emitted particles should drift even without audio
- The face should not completely deform beyond recognition

Low reaction:

- Low-sensitive emitted particles should burst outward more strongly from the mouth
- Some facial outline elements may pulse locally
- The whole face must not just scale up and down as one object

Mid reaction:

- Mid-sensitive features should wobble, flex, or ripple locally
- The smile curve can become more animated
- Emitted particles can gain turbulence
- Do not rotate the entire face as one parent group

High reaction:

- Eye and mouth nodes can sparkle
- A changing subset of emitted particles can brighten and flicker
- Add short glints to facial points and particle trails
- Avoid full-scene flashing

Shared controls:

- Visual Density controls emitter particle count and structural node count
- Motion Speed controls resting motion and emitted particle drift
- Element Size controls outline point size / line weight impression / particle size
- Depth controls 3D separation and emitted particle depth spread
- Glow controls luminous intensity
- Palette controls colour scheme

TEMPLATE 2: MUSHROOM PULSE

Create a recognisable mushroom shape.

This should be a stylised psychedelic or geometric mushroom silhouette, not a photoreal mushroom.

Visual idea:

A central mushroom with:

- clear cap
- clear stem
- optional underside gill-like or radial structures
- geometric or particle detailing
- some 3D depth so it is not a flat icon

It should be unmistakably a mushroom when paused.

Construction guidance:

- Cap may be built from a domed point field, curved line structure, or layered geometry
- Stem may be a structured column or particle / wireframe form
- Optional spores or particles may drift around it
- Underside details can help give it character
- The template should feel a bit surreal or trippy, but not messy

Base movement:

- The mushroom can gently breathe or pulse in place
- Spores / ambient particles can drift around it
- Small cap undulations or subtle layered movement are welcome
- The shape must remain recognisable

Low reaction:

- Low-sensitive cap regions can pulse or throb locally
- Some spores or particles can get pushed outward
- Stem structure may show restrained local pressure or deformation
- Do not scale the whole mushroom as one rigid object

Mid reaction:

- Mid-sensitive gill, cap, or contour structures can ripple
- Add flowing movement across the cap surface
- Spores can gain more dynamic swirling motion
- Do not rotate the whole mushroom root as the main effect

High reaction:

- Spores and cap-edge details can sparkle
- Small bright flickers can appear on cap points or around the silhouette
- Selected particle regions can twinkle
- Avoid flashing the entire mushroom or canvas

Shared controls:

- Visual Density controls detail level, spore count, and structural complexity
- Motion Speed controls ambient drift and contour motion
- Element Size controls mushroom detail size and particle size
- Depth controls mushroom thickness and spore depth spread
- Glow controls luminous intensity
- Palette controls colours

TEMPLATE 3: REACTIVE EYE

Create a proper eye shape.

This must be a clear eye with:

- outer eye shape
- iris
- pupil
- visible central gaze structure

Do not make this a vague almond outline.
Do not make it just a ring.
Do not make it merely “eye-like”.

It needs to read clearly as an eye.

Visual idea:

A stylised, high-contrast, reactive eye built from layered geometry with strong central focus.

The pupil and iris must be obvious.

Construction guidance:

- Outer eye shape can be built from line or particle contours
- Iris should be a distinct circular or radially structured region
- Pupil should be a clearly darker or more concentrated central area
- Additional radial detail around the iris is encouraged
- The eye can have concentric or wireframe layering
- It should work extremely well with Kaleidoscope

Base movement:

- The eye should have subtle living motion even when paused
- Iris detail can shimmer or rotate locally
- Outer contour can drift subtly
- The eye must remain recognisable and centered

Low reaction:

- Low-sensitive iris and contour regions can pulse outward locally
- Some surrounding particles can be pushed away from the eye
- Do not scale the whole eye uniformly

Mid reaction:

- Mid-sensitive iris structures can twist, ripple, or show radial turbulence
- The pupil edge and iris detail can animate independently
- Add local wave motion across iris lines
- Do not rotate the full eye root as the main effect

High reaction:

- The pupil edge, iris highlights, and eyelid points can sparkle
- Selected radial features can flicker brightly
- Add controlled glints around the eye
- Avoid full-canvas flashing

Optional good behaviour:

- The pupil can subtly dilate with some band influence if done tastefully
- If added, this must be local and controlled, not cartoonish

Shared controls:

- Visual Density controls iris detail, outer contour detail, and surrounding particles
- Motion Speed controls subtle living motion
- Element Size controls line weight impression and node scale
- Depth controls layered separation and radial thickness
- Glow controls luminous intensity
- Palette controls colours

TEMPLATE ARCHITECTURE

Preserve the current template architecture:

- one persistent WebGLRenderer
- one visible renderer canvas
- one scene
- one camera
- one animation loop
- one ResizeObserver
- one active template root at a time

Each template should continue exposing a generic THREE.Object3D root and update method.

When switching templates:

1. remove previous template root from scene
2. dispose old template resources
3. create new template instance
4. add new root to scene
5. continue using existing renderer, camera, animation loop, and recording stream

Do not create another canvas, renderer, scene, or animation loop.
