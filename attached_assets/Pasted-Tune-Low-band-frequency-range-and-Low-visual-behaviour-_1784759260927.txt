GOAL

Make Low feel tighter, more physical, and more spatial.

Low should now mean:

- true kick/sub pressure
- physical separation
- expansion
- breathing
- space opening up between elements

Low should not mainly mean random brightness, chaotic movement, or whole-scene shaking.

CHANGE 1: LOW FREQUENCY RANGE

Update the audio frequency bands.

New ranges:

Low:
20 Hz to 100 Hz

Ignored gap:
100 Hz to 350 Hz

Mid:
350 Hz to 4,000 Hz

High:
4,000 Hz to 16,000 Hz

The 100 Hz to 350 Hz range should not feed Low, Mid, or High.

This gap is intentional.

Update the central frequency band definitions only.

Do not hardcode FFT bin numbers.

Continue deriving bin indices from:

- AudioContext sample rate
- fftSize
- frequency range in Hz

Both audio modes must use the new ranges:

- uploaded track mode
- live input mode

Both signal types must use the new ranges:

- level
- hit / spectral flux

If UI labels show old ranges, update them.

Expected UI labels:

Low: 20-100 Hz
Mid: 350-4,000 Hz
High: 4,000-16,000 Hz

Because Low now has fewer bins, lightly check level and hit sensitivity if needed.

Do not add new sensitivity controls.

Low should still respond clearly to kick and sub-heavy bass.

CHANGE 2: LOW BEHAVIOUR PRINCIPLE

Retune Low mapping across templates.

Low should generally create:

- separation between elements
- expansion of forms
- spatial breathing
- outward pressure
- deeper spacing
- physical punch

Low should not make templates chaotic.

Low should not flash the whole scene.

Low should not rotate the whole root as the main effect.

Low should not simply brighten everything.

At Low Influence 100%:

- Low should be clearly visible on kick/sub hits
- the template should remain composed

At Low Influence 200%:

- Low should feel strong and physical
- the template should still remain usable

At Low Influence 0%:

- Low should have no visible effect
- resting animation should continue

TEMPLATE-SPECIFIC CHANGES

CUBE SWARM

The user may refer to this as Cube Storm, but the existing template is Cube Swarm.

Low behaviour should change so the cubes move away from each other.

Desired behaviour:

- On Low Hit, cubes should create more space between themselves.
- Cubes should push outward from the swarm center, or away from their local cluster center.
- The effect should feel like the swarm briefly expands.
- Cubes should not all move identically.
- Preserve cube tumbling and existing Mid/High behaviour.
- Avoid making the whole root scale uniformly.

Implementation idea:

- Store each cube's base position or base radial direction.
- Add a bounded Low offset along each cube's outward direction.
- Use Low Hit for punch and Low Level for slower pressure.
- Let the offset decay naturally as the hit envelope decays.

LASER LATTICE

Low behaviour should make the lines move away from each other and create space.

Desired behaviour:

- On Low Hit, line groups should separate more clearly.
- Lines should spread outward from the center or from their local lanes.
- The lattice should feel like it opens up under kick pressure.
- Preserve the depth travel.
- Preserve the cleaner reduced default density.
- Do not make the lines chaotic.
- Do not just brighten all lines.

Implementation idea:

- Give each line or line group an outward lane direction.
- Add a bounded Low separation offset.
- Low Hit creates quick spacing.

Implementation idea:
