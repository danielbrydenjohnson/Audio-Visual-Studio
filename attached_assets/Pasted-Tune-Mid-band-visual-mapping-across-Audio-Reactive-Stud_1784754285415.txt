GOAL

The Mid band currently behaves inconsistently.

On some templates it has a mild useful effect.

On other templates it drives the visuals absolutely mad.

Fix this by retuning the visual mapping of Mid Level and Mid Hit across all current templates.

CORE MEANING OF EACH BAND

Preserve this visual language:

Low = weight, impact, pressure

Low should drive:

- kick/bass punch
- outward pressure
- depth surges
- heavy pulses
- physical movement

High = sparkle, detail, sharpness

High should drive:

- glints
- flickers
- sparkles
- edge highlights
- small fast details

Mid = body, rhythm, articulation

Mid should drive:

- controlled local shape modulation
- subtle bend
- mild twist
- ripple
- phase movement
- musical articulation

Mid must not be the chaos band.

PROBLEM TO FIX

Mid is too aggressive in some templates.

Likely causes:

- Mid Level mapped to large rotation
- Mid Level mapped to strong turbulence
- Mid Hit adding accumulating angular velocity
- Mid Hit causing unbounded phase drift
- Mid affecting too many elements at once
- Mid moving entire structures too much
- Mid making templates lose their shape

Fix the template mappings so Mid remains composed.

IMPORTANT RULES

At Mid Influence 100%:

- every template should remain visually composed
- Mid should be noticeable but tasteful
- no template should spin, thrash, explode, or become unreadable

At Mid Influence 200%:

- Mid can become energetic
- Mid should still remain usable
- no template should become visually broken
- no template should become chaotic noise

At Mid Influence 0%:

- Mid Level and Mid Hit should have no visible effect
- resting animation should continue

Mid effects should be bounded.

Do not let Mid Hit permanently accumulate uncontrolled speed, spin, offset, or distortion.

If Mid Hit currently adds angular velocity, change it to a temporary bounded impulse or local offset that returns to normal naturally.

Do not rotate the full template root as the main Mid effect.

Do not scale the full scene as the main Mid effect.

Do not flash the full canvas.

TEMPLATE-SPECIFIC TUNING

Apply this to every current working template.

CUBE SWARM

Current issue to avoid:
- Mid should not make cubes spin insanely or tumble out of control.

Desired Mid behaviour:
- Mid Level creates mild per-cube wobble or orientation variation.
- Mid Hit creates a short tumbling accent on selected cubes.
- The burst should be bounded and decay naturally.
- Cubes should remain readable.

Reduce:
- accumulated angular velocity from Mid
- global tumble strength
- excessive rotation multipliers

LASER LATTICE

Current issue to avoid:
- Mid should not make the lattice kink wildly or shred the structure.

Desired Mid behaviour:
- Mid Level creates subtle local phase shifts or controlled directional bends.
- Mid Hit creates a brief local kink or ripple through selected line groups.
- The lattice should still feel like a clean spatial structure.

Reduce:
- large direction changes
- excessive turbulence
- too many lines affected at once
- unbounded phase distortion

WIREFRAME BLOOM

Current issue to avoid:
- Mid should not make shells thrash or spin too fast.

Desired Mid behaviour:
- Mid Level gently twists or offsets layers.
- Mid Hit briefly accelerates unfolding or adds a controlled layer twist.
- The bloom should remain elegant and readable.

Reduce:
- spin bursts
- large rotational impulses
- full-form twisting
- anything that causes shape collapse

FIBONACCI SPIRAL

Desired Mid behaviour:

IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

Prefer:

- smaller Mid multipliers
- bounded sine/cosine offsets
- temporary offsets based directly on current Mid Hit envelope
- local phase shifts
- per-element variation with restrained amplitude
- clamp values where needed
- reduce number of elements affected by Mid Hit if required

Avoid:

- adding Mid Hit directly to permanent velocity without damping
- applying Mid to root transforms
- huge turbulence values
- huge rotation multipliers
- all elements reacting identically
- all elements reacting at full strength
- full-scene flashing

If a template already has a good Mid response, only lightly tune it.

Do not make every template identical.

Each template can keep its own character, but Mid should consistently mean controlled articulation rather than chaos.
